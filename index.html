Complete Filtration Ecosystems

Dry filtration

Dry Filtration is the process of removing solid particles from air or gas streams using filter media such as filter cloths, cartridges, or bags—without the use of liquids. It is commonly used in industries like cement, and metal processing.

Wet filtration

Wet Filtration is the process of separating solids from liquids using filter media like filter cloths or filter presses. It is widely used in industries such as food processing, pharmaceuticals, mining, and wastewater treatment to clarify liquids or recover valuable solids

Filter media

Filter Cloths are specially woven or non-woven fabrics used in industrial filtration systems to separate solids from liquids or gases. They are designed for durability, chemical resistance, and efficient particle retention

Filter housings and Filter cartridges

Filter housings hold cartridges that remove particles and contaminants from liquids or gases. This setup ensures clean output, protects equipment, and is used in industries like water treatment, food processing, and manufacturing.
